Kamila Valieva Biography, Early Life, Career

Valieva began skating with RSDUSSHOR in Kazan in 2009. Ksenia Ivanova was her first coach, followed by Marina Kudriavtseva, Igor Lyutikov, and Natalia Dubinskaya. She relocated to Moscow when she was six years old to train at SSHOR Moskvich. Valieva switched skating clubs in the spring of 2018 to Sambo-70, when Eteri Tutberidze agreed to let her join her group. Prior to her international debut in the 2018–19 season, recordings of Valieva's short performance, performed to Arvo Pärt's Spiegel im Spiegel and were inspired by Pablo Picasso's painting Girl on a Ball, attracted widespread notice. Tutberidze called it her favorite show of the year. The show also caught the attention of Picasso's granddaughter Diana Widmaier Picasso, who asked Valieva to visit her in Paris. Among her many successes that season was the Russian Younger Age Championships, which she won ahead of training partners Sofia Akatieva and Sofia Samodelkina. Valieva later declared that she will maintain her short program for her international debut next season.
