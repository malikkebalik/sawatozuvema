Who is Amy Mickelson?

Popularly known as the great golfer Phil Mickelson‘s wife, Amy is a Hollywood actress and a social worker.

Together with her husband, the couple has established Phil and Amy’s foundation to donate to the needy and specialize in family and youth initiatives.

Amy Mickelson was born in 1972; her exact date of birth is hidden from the public for an unknown reason.

She was born in California, United States; again, the exact details of her birth are undisclosed for whatever reason.

Which institutions did Amy Mickelson attend? | Educational Background

When she was in her mid-teens, Amy attended Harvest Park Middle School, located at Valley Ave, Pleasanton, California.

Following that, she was the freshman princess of Foothill high school in 1986.

Dave Ferry, a former student at the same institution and Greatest Man on Campus (GMOC) in his freshman year, remembers Mickelson.

He stated that Amy’s sincere, sweet behavior overlooked her beauty.

Dave said that he still remembers the first day Amy came to school. He said that all the popular boys would be in their bunch during lunchtime, and all the popular girls would have their group.

“All the boys went mingling over by her. So the popular girls panicked, introduced themselves, and all was right with the world,” Dave further commented.

Eventually, Amy Mickelson moved to Hillcrest High School, located in Midvale, Utah. She graduated from Hillcrest in 1990.

Regarding her college education, initially, she attended the University of Utah but did not last long there.

She transferred to Arizona State University, where she met her husband of 27 years.

Amy Mickelson’s Career

During her time at Arizona State University, Amy used to be a cheerleader. Amy was a cheerleader for the Phoenix Suns of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

Since her days at the Arizona State, she aspired to become an actress.

Following her passion for acting, she debuted in a film entitled Sunset (1988). Sadly, the movie failed to make an impression. 

Apparently, that was the end of her career as an actress. Perhaps her acting skills were not that great, or maybe she found something more interesting than acting.

Mickelson now works as a social worker and runs a foundation named after herself and her husband.

The pro golfer’s wife has also established a foundation that raises funds for breast cancer women.

Battling Breast Cancer

In 2009, Amy was diagnosed with deadly breast cancer. By the middle of 2009, she had begun her cancer treatment.

Following her treatment, she moved to Houston, Texas, for further medication and therapy.

In the late May of 2009, Amy’s diagnosis became public; Phil put a hold on his golf career for a moment there.

He opted out from defending his title the following weekend.

Mickelson wrote that there is an excellent team of doctors working on improving Amy’s condition. “We caught this early,” wrote Phil.

Phil also wrote that the couple is waiting for the test results to guide them in the best possible direction.

Amy had never missed any of Phil’s matches; she always stood by his side, cheering him and supporting him.

But her battle with breast cancer stopped her from doing so for a while.

However, Amy did show up at the 2010 Masters Tournament Finals, where Phil was playing against Lee Westwood. The event was held at the Augusta National Golf Course.

Amy, along with her three children, showed support and encouraged Phil. Likewise, Phil Mickelson went on to snatch the trophy and won a 1.35 million dollars prize.

In 2012, she started attending Phil’s matches after her health conditions began showing signs of improvement.

Her health got significantly better, and she eventually recovered from breast cancer.

The 2012 Ryder Cup was the first match she attended after her recovery.

Support for Breast Cancer at Phil’s Match

During the 2009 PGA tour, Amy’s husband, Phil Mickelson, and his colleagues and their wives wore pink in Amy’s honor. As a result, the event was named ‘Pink Out.’

This special honorary event was held in Fort Worth, Texas, at the Crowne Plaza Invitational at Colonial.

Tears were streaming down the couple’s face when they heard that the Crowne Plaza Invitational at Colonial, PGA Tour, and tour wives organized the event.

“We both had tears rolling down our faces,” said Phil during an interview.

He added that the ‘Pink Out’ was an incredibly thoughtful gesture for Amy and the two hundred thousand women who have breast cancer each year.

Phil and Amy Mickelson Foundation

The Phil and Amy Mickelson Foundation recently donated a million dollars to 14 charity organizations in Coachella valley.

The proceedings from the 2020 American Express tournament were donated.

American Express tournament was held in the January of 2020, which successfully raised over a million dollars. Various charities, including the Boys and Girls Club of Coachella Valley, Alzheimer’s Association Coachella Valley, and Coachella Valley Rescue Mission, received the donation.
