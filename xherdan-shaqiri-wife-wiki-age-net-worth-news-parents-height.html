Xherdan Shaqiri  Career

Shaqiri began his career at his hometown club, SV August, and was noticed by FC Basel just at age of eight.   He played in the Under-15 Nike Cup 2007 for Basel's young squad and was voted the tournament's top player. Despite interest from other teams, he chose to remain with Basel, when he played for their reserve team in the Swiss 1. Liga from 2007 to 2009. Two years later, on 2 January 2009, Shaqiri signed a first professional deal with Basel, which kept them at St. Jakob-Park until December 2011. 

In a 2-1 victory over Manchester United in December 2011, Shaqiri contributed two assists.  For the second time in his Basel career, he won a Double, consisting of the League title and the Swiss Cup, at the end of the 2011-12 season.

Shaqiri made his under-21 debut for Switzerland on November 11, 2009, in a 3-1 win over Turkey in a 2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualifier. He scored his first goal for them against Denmark in the opening game of a 2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship on June 11, 2011. 

Shaqiri was promoted to Switzerland's senior team in 2010 and made his debut in a 3-1 friendly loss to Uruguay on March 3, 2010. After an unexpected call-up by manager Ottmar Hitzfeld, he was named to the 2010 World Cup team. On September 7, 2010, Shaqiri scored his first goal for them, a long-range left-footed strike in a 1-3 loss to England in a Euro 2012 qualifier. On September 6, 2011, he scored a hat-trick as Switzerland rallied from behind to defeat Bulgaria 3-1 in another Euro 2012 qualifier.   Switzerland also sought to choose him for the Olympic Football Tournament in 2012, but he chose to stay at his current club for pre-season training.

Shaqiri was named to Switzerland's 23-man roster for such 2018 FIFA World Cup, which is being hosted in Russia. He scored the game-winning goal as Switzerland rallied from behind to overcome Serbia 2-1 in the group stage. He and his teammate, Kosovar-born Granit Xhaka, celebrated their goals with only an eagle gesture, a symbol for ethnic Albanians. Switzerland captain Stephan Lichtsteiner, who isn't really of Albanian ancestry, made the identical motion to celebrate Shaqiri's goal and afterward supported both players. Xhaka and Shaqiri were fined 10,000 Swiss francs by FIFA for "unsporting behavior according to the rules of fair play," while Lichtsteiner was fined 5,000 Swiss francs.
